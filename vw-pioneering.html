VW Pioneering Research — Scratchpad

The Utility Triad Beyond the
Western Lab

What does the tradeoff space between analytic power, privacy, and synthesis look like outside the research system that built the infrastructure?

Core research question
The unknown unknown

Multimodal behavioral research infrastructure — pose estimation, audio processing, gesture detection, de-identification pipelines — was built in Western labs on Western data. It encodes assumptions about whose bodies, whose languages, and whose consent models are default. The utility triad (AU/RU/GU) makes those assumptions legible in the Western context. The genuinely unknown question is: what is the shape of that tradeoff space in non-Western settings, and can it be formally characterised?

VW framing: This is not gap-filling research. It is the kind of question that cannot be answered by extending existing methods — because the methods are themselves part of what needs to be examined. That is what "exploring the unknown unknown" means here.
Three pillars — research programme structure
AU · WP1

Analytic utility across systems

Systematically benchmark SMASH/EnvisionBOX pipeline performance on Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo speech and gesture corpora. Quantify the analytic utility gap between Western and non-Western inputs. Build the first cross-cultural multimodal benchmark.

WP1 · yrs 1–2 · lead: Owoyele + de Melo (HPI)
RU · WP2

Governance models that fit

Individual IRB consent does not map onto community-based data practices in West African research contexts. Develop and validate community consent frameworks for multimodal research data. Extend MaskBench to non-Western facial and body morphology.

WP2 · yrs 1–3 · lead: Owoyele + Pouw (Tilburg)
GU · WP3

Synthetic data for underrepresented populations

88% of African languages absent from modern LLM training. Design governance frameworks for synthetic multimodal corpus generation in low-resource settings. What can be synthesized — and when does synthesis compensate versus harm?

WP3 · yrs 2–5 · lead: Owoyele + de Melo (HPI/JEGAL)
Positionality argument
Why this researcher

As a Nigerian researcher building infrastructure that currently fails Nigerian data, this is not a study of underrepresented populations from the outside. It is the construction of tools by someone whose own linguistic and cultural context the existing infrastructure does not serve. That is a distinctive epistemic position — and one the VW Pioneering Research programme is designed to fund. The NL–DE–NG axis (Radboud/Tilburg → HPI Potsdam / Leuphana → Nigerian field sites) is already partly in place through the LEGO Foundation collaboration and the SLlama low-resource language work.
