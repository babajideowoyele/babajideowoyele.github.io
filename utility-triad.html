Three vertices.
One infrastructure.

Most discussions treat analytic power and privacy compliance as opposing forces. The utility triad reframes this: AU, RU, and GU are interdependent dimensions of what mature multimodal infrastructure must address.

SMASH is the reference implementation — operationalising all three tiers through its canonical event log, MaskBench benchmarks, and JEGAL embedding substrate.

AU — Analytic Utility

Extract maximum signal · enable novel research design

RU — Reflexive Utility

Privacy & de-identification · accessible to SSH early career researchers

GU — Generative Utility

Synthetic data · model training · deepfakes · responsible by design

Three vertices

AU · Analytic Utility

Analytic Utility

The signal extractable from a corpus using available analytic methods. Every pipeline decision — masking, segmentation, extraction, alignment — either preserves or reduces it. Maximising AU enables novel research designs: cross-corpus comparisons, longitudinal kinematic tracking, gesture–prosody synchrony analysis at scale.

pose estimation speech features kinematic sync novel research design
RU · Reflexive Utility

Reflexive Utility

Privacy and de-identification made accessible to SSH early career researchers. RU is the capacity to reason explicitly about what each masking choice costs in analytic quality — so researchers balance rather than toggle. SMASH operationalises RU through MaskBench benchmarks and provenance metadata that travel with every event in the log, lowering the barrier for ECRs who need to share video corpora responsibly.

de-identification MaskBench provenance ECR-accessible FAIR
GU · Generative Utility

Generative Utility

The capacity to synthesize new data and representations using generative AI. Use cases span synthetic data generation, model training on privacy-preserved corpora, and — at the risk frontier — deepfake production that responsible governance must anticipate. The most powerful tier and the most governance-demanding.

Theoretical grounding

Extending Maass et al.
into multimodal
infrastructure

The utility triad extends the data-driven / theory-driven framework of Maass, Parsons, Purao, Storey & Woo (2018, JAIS) into the specific context of multimodal data infrastructure. Their four IS challenges map cleanly onto the three vertices: AU addresses challenges 1 and 2 (knowledge creation and theory testing); RU addresses challenge 4 (sharing under privacy constraints); GU opens a fifth challenge that was beyond 2018 scope — synthesis.

The base edge of the triangle — RU ↔ GU — is the governance frontier: as generative AI increases the power to produce synthetic corpora, the reflexive capacity to audit and constrain that production becomes existentially important. Infrastructure that operationalises only AU without RU is incomplete; infrastructure that enables GU without RU is dangerous.

A preprint formally introducing the framework is in preparation.
